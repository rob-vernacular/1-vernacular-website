Pillar 01
Cultural Intelligence
What it is

Not cultural sensitivity training. Deep, practical exploration of how culture shapes thought, behaviour, communication and power — with Africa and Ethiopia specifically at the centre.

Modules
The Addis Vernacular: A Diplomat's Immersion
Full day8–12 peopleField-based
Decoding the unspoken context of Addis. Coffee culture, high-context hierarchies, what your pre-departure briefing got wrong. Morning briefing followed by a guided city scavenger hunt.
High-Context Leadership: The AU and UNECA Code
4 hours15–20 peopleCase studies
How to read power, read rooms and read processes inside AU/UN institutions. The molecular structure of pan-African decision-making.
Reading the Room
Full dayUp to 20Roleplay
How power, time, silence, disagreement and trust differ across cultures — and how to adapt in real time.
The Africa Lens
Full dayUp to 20Structured debate
For global organisations whose HQ is elsewhere. Shifts default Western narratives about Africa with perspective and structured debate — not lectures.
Protocol and Presence
Half dayUp to 25Interactive briefing
The unwritten rules. Communication, hierarchy, hospitality, negotiation, religious calendar. Specific to Ethiopia and East Africa.

Most institutional communication is dry, defensive and donor-facing. This pillar teaches narrative as a strategic asset — not a soft skill — that determines funding, trust and impact perception.

Modules

2 days10–15 peopleStudio workshop

The Briefing Masterclass
3 hoursUp to 30Spark Sprint
How to write briefs that actually spark innovation rather than just compliance. The art of distilling complex problems into single-sentence challenges.
Brand for Good
Full dayUp to 18Strategy workshop
How mission-driven organisations treat brand as a strategic asset. Shapes trust, funding, staff attraction and community credibility.

AI, Tech & the Future

The pressure to "do something about AI" is everywhere. Most AI training ignores African data ethics, development context and the specific workflows of NGOs and diplomatic institutions. We don't.

Full day20–25 peopleHands-on session
Using GenAI for programme planning, budgeting and reporting — with a focus on African data ethics. Real-time prompting using actual project data. No slide decks.
Digital Diplomacy: Engaging the Youth Vernacular
6 hours12–15 peopleFuture-Casting
Using tech and social subcultures to reach Gen Z across Ethiopia and the continent. Collaborative session with young local Imagineers from the Vernacular network.
